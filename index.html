Welcome

I'm a Ph.D. student in the Computational Science program at Middle Tennessee State University. I received my bachelor of science degree in Physics from Truman State University in 2012 and master of science degree in Computer Science from Middle Tennessee State University in 2015. I am originally from Nepal. It is a small but beautiful country in South Asia. I moved to the States in 2008 for my undergraduate studies.

My research interests are in computational photonics, acoustics and Monte Carlo simulations of light transport in scattering media. I am currently working on computational modeling of Bloch surface waves - strongly confined electromagnetic exitation at the surface of a dielectric multilayer structure - in one-dimensional periodic and aperiodic photonic bandgap materials. These waves can be used to make higly sensitive protein-antibody binding detection bio-sensors, fluorescence and label-free bio-sensors, and surface enhanced Raman spectroscopy devices. I use Rigorous Coupled Wave Analysis (RCWA) method, Finite Difference Time Domain (FDTD) method, and Finite Element (FE) method for my research. Besides using commercial (COMSOL Multiphysics, Lumerical Solutions) and open-source (Meep) software, I have also implemeted a 3D scattering matrix based RCWA method and FDTD method in C++ and MATLAB.
