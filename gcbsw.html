Grating coupled BSW

Grating coupling [see Figure 1] is an alternate Bloch surface wave coupling technique that relaxes the parallel wave vector conservation condition at the surface and essentially permits light to be diffracted into surface modes without the use of a prism. As shown in the inset of Figure 1, in the presence of surface gratings on a 1DPFM, the incident light is diffracted into several diffraction orders (m= ±1,±2,⋯).These diffraction orders from the grating can provide the additional momentum to the incident light required to couple to BSWs as given by km = kinc ± mG, where km is the wave number of the diffracted beam, kinc = k0 sin(θinc) is the parallel component of the incident wave number, m is the diffraction order, and G = 2π/Λy is the grating wave number, Λy being the grating period.

The advantage of using grating coupling technique over prism coupling technique is two fold. First, it mitigates the requirement of a bulky prism, which in turn makes is appropriate for making nanoscale biosensors and circuits. Second, due to the grating profile on the surface layer, it can be used for azimuthal angular interrogation. We study one-dimensional periodic multilayer structures with grating profile on the surface. The ability to sweep over the azimuthal angle can be effectively used for making sensitivity enhanced biosensors.
